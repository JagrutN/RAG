Statistical Learning Overview
Statistical Learning is a field of study that focuses on developing models and algorithms to understand patterns in data, make predictions, and infer relationships between variables. It is a core discipline in machine learning, heavily based on statistics and probability theory.

Statistical learning methods can be categorized into supervised and unsupervised learning, with applications in areas like regression, classification, clustering, and dimensionality reduction.

Key Concepts in Statistical Learning
1. Supervised Learning (Labeled Data)
Regression – Predicting continuous values (e.g., housing prices, stock prices).
Methods: Linear Regression, Ridge Regression, Lasso Regression, Polynomial Regression
Classification – Predicting categorical outcomes (e.g., spam detection, disease diagnosis).
Methods: Logistic Regression, Decision Trees, Support Vector Machines (SVMs), Naïve Bayes
2. Unsupervised Learning (Unlabeled Data)
Clustering – Grouping similar data points together.
Methods: K-Means, Hierarchical Clustering, DBSCAN
Dimensionality Reduction – Reducing data complexity while retaining essential features.
Methods: Principal Component Analysis (PCA), Linear Discriminant Analysis (LDA), t-SNE
3. Bias-Variance Tradeoff
Bias: Error due to overly simplistic models that fail to capture data complexity (underfitting).
Variance: Error due to overly complex models that capture noise as patterns (overfitting).
Goal: Find the optimal balance for the best generalization.
4. Model Selection & Validation
Cross-Validation – Splitting data into training/testing sets to avoid overfitting.
Regularization – Techniques like L1 (Lasso) and L2 (Ridge) to prevent overfitting.
5. Probabilistic Foundations
Bayes’ Theorem – Used in Naïve Bayes classifiers and Bayesian inference.
Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE) – Finding model parameters that maximize data likelihood.
Markov Models – Used in time-series forecasting and NLP applications.
Applications of Statistical Learning
Finance – Stock price prediction, fraud detection, risk assessment.
Healthcare – Disease classification, personalized medicine, medical image analysis.
Marketing – Customer segmentation, recommendation systems.
Natural Language Processing – Sentiment analysis, topic modeling.
Computer Vision – Object recognition, facial recognition.
